## This Week's Goals

- Check in and out of hotels smoothly
- Request specific room types and amenities
- Ask about hotel facilities and services
- Report room problems and request assistance
- Handle payment and receipts

## Grammar Focus

### Making Requests and Preferences

**Rule:** Use "Queria..." (I'd like...) for polite requests and "Prefiro..." (I prefer...) for preferences.

Examples:
- "Queria um quarto não fumante" (I'd like a non-smoking room)
- "Prefiro um quarto com vista" (I prefer a room with a view)
- "Pode me dar um recibo?" (Can you give me a receipt?)

### Asking About Availability and Services

**Rule:** Use "Tem...?" (Is there...? / Do you have...?) for asking about facilities and services.

Examples:
- "Tem Wi-Fi?" (Is there WiFi?)
- "O café da manhã está incluído?" (Is breakfast included?)
- "Tem quarto disponível?" (Do you have an available room?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In with a Reservation

Practice checking in at the hotel front desk with a confirmed reservation, providing your details, and getting your room key.

Key phrases:
- "Tenho uma reserva" (I have a reservation)
- "Meu nome é..." (My name is...)
- "O número da reserva é..." (The reservation number is...)
- "Aqui está meu passaporte" (Here is my passport)
- "O café da manhã está incluído?" (Is breakfast included?)

### Requesting a Room Without Reservation

Practice asking about room availability, discussing room types and prices, and making a booking on the spot.

Key phrases:
- "Tem quarto disponível?" (Do you have an available room?)
- "Quanto custa por noite?" (How much per night?)
- "Queria um quarto não fumante" (I'd like a non-smoking room)
- "Tem Wi-Fi?" (Is there WiFi?)

### Reporting Room Problems

Practice reporting issues with your room (broken AC, no hot water, noisy neighbors) and requesting assistance or a room change.

Key phrases:
- "Tem um problema" (There's a problem)
- "O ar condicionado não funciona" (The air conditioning doesn't work)
- "Não tem água quente" (There's no hot water)
- "Quero trocar de quarto" (I want to change rooms)

### Checking Out and Payment

Practice checking out, reviewing charges, requesting a receipt, and asking about luggage storage.

Key phrases:
- "Quero fazer check-out" (I want to check out)
- "Vou pagar no cartão" (I'll pay by card)
- "Pode me dar o recibo?" (Can you give me the receipt?)
- "Pode guardar minha bagagem?" (Can you hold my luggage?)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in with a reservation
- Negotiate room type and price without a reservation
- Report a room problem and get it resolved
- Ask about hotel facilities and breakfast times
- Check out and request a receipt and luggage storage

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Quarto bom" → "Um quarto bom" (always add article!)
- "Tem Wi-Fi?" spoken casually → Perfectly acceptable in Brazil - casual is normal
- "Toalha, por favor" → "Pode me dar uma toalha?" (more polite to ask "can you give me")
- Speaking without "Com licença" → Start with "Com licença" for politeness
- "Café da manhã é que horas?" → "A que horas é o café da manhã?" (correct question word order)
- Not saying "obrigado/obrigada" → Always thank staff (-o for men, -a for women)
- Being too formal → Brazilians are warm and informal - friendly tone works well

## Cultural Notes

- **Warm hospitality**: Brazilian hotels are known for warm, friendly service. Staff are personable and helpful - enjoy the relaxed atmosphere.
- **Breakfast is substantial**: "Café da manhã" in Brazilian hotels is often a large buffet with fruits, bread, cheese, eggs, juice, and coffee. Don't miss it!
- **Late check-in**: Hotels are generally flexible with late arrivals. Call if you'll arrive very late (after midnight).
- **Key/card return**: Some hotels ask you to leave key at front desk when going out. Ask "Deixo a chave aqui?" (Do I leave the key here?).
- **Breakfast times**: Usually 7-10 AM. Missing it means you miss it - no refunds. Set an alarm if you're a late sleeper.
- **Check-out time**: Usually 12 noon, sometimes flexible. Late check-out (late check-out) may cost extra or be free if you ask nicely.
- **Luggage storage**: Hotels will hold luggage after check-out for free. Say "Pode guardar minha bagagem?" (Can you hold my luggage?).
- **Payment**: Most hotels accept credit cards, but some smaller pousadas prefer cash. Ask "Aceita cartão?" (Do you accept cards?) when checking in.
- **Tipping culture**: 10% service charge is often included in hotel restaurants. Additional tipping is optional but appreciated for good service.
- **No excessive formality**: Unlike some cultures, Brazilians prefer informal, friendly interaction. Smile, be warm, use "você" (you) naturally.
- **Room size**: Brazilian hotel rooms are generally spacious, especially outside major cities. Enjoy the space!
- **Friendly chat expected**: Brazilian hotel staff will often chat warmly with you. This is normal friendliness, not pushiness. Engage if you'd like!
