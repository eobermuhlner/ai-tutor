## This Week's Goals

- Master regular preterite conjugations (-ar, -er, -ir verbs)
- Talk about completed past actions (what you did yesterday, last week)
- Use time expressions to frame past events
- Understand the difference between preterite and present tense

## Grammar Focus

### The Preterite Tense (Pretérito)

**Rule:** The preterite is used for completed actions in the past with a definite beginning and end

Use preterite when:
- Talking about specific completed actions: "Ayer comí pizza" (Yesterday I ate pizza)
- Describing events that happened at a specific time: "Anoche estudié dos horas" (Last night I studied two hours)
- Narrating a sequence of events: "Llegué, comí, y dormí" (I arrived, ate, and slept)

### Regular -AR Verb Conjugations

**Pattern:** Remove -ar, add preterite endings

| Person | Ending | Example: hablar (to speak) |
|--------|--------|---------------------------|
| Yo | -é | hablé (I spoke) |
| Tú | -aste | hablaste (you spoke) |
| Él/Ella/Usted | -ó | habló (he/she/you spoke) |
| Nosotros | -amos | hablamos (we spoke) |
| Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes | -aron | hablaron (they/you all spoke) |

**Note:** "Nosotros" form looks identical to present tense - use context!

Examples:
- Trabajé mucho ayer (I worked a lot yesterday)
- ¿Estudiaste anoche? (Did you study last night?)
- Mi hermano caminó al parque (My brother walked to the park)

### Regular -ER and -IR Verb Conjugations

**Pattern:** Remove -er/-ir, add preterite endings (SAME endings for both!)

| Person | Ending | Example: comer (to eat) | Example: vivir (to live) |
|--------|--------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Yo | -í | comí (I ate) | viví (I lived) |
| Tú | -iste | comiste (you ate) | viviste (you lived) |
| Él/Ella/Usted | -ió | comió (he/she ate) | vivió (he/she lived) |
| Nosotros | -imos | comimos (we ate) | vivimos (we lived) |
| Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes | -ieron | comieron (they ate) | vivieron (they lived) |

Examples:
- Bebí café esta mañana (I drank coffee this morning)
- Mis padres vivieron en Madrid (My parents lived in Madrid)
- ¿Saliste anoche? (Did you go out last night?)

### Spelling Changes with -AR Verbs

**Rule:** Some -ar verbs need spelling adjustments to preserve pronunciation

- Verbs ending in -car: c → qu before é
  - Buscar → yo busqué (I searched)
  - Tocar → yo toqué (I played/touched)

- Verbs ending in -gar: g → gu before é
  - Llegar → yo llegué (I arrived)
  - Jugar → yo jugué (I played)

- Verbs ending in -zar: z → c before é
  - Empezar → yo empecé (I started)
  - Almorzar → yo almorcé (I had lunch)

**Important:** These changes only happen in the "yo" form!

### Time Expressions for Past

**Always use these with preterite:**

- Ayer (yesterday)
- Anoche (last night)
- Ayer por la mañana/tarde/noche (yesterday morning/afternoon/night)
- La semana pasada (last week)
- El mes pasado (last month)
- El año pasado (last year)
- Hace... (ago)
  - Hace dos días (two days ago)
  - Hace una semana (a week ago)

- **Ayer por la mañana** - Yesterday morning
- **La semana pasada** - Last week

- **Hace mucho tiempo** - A long time ago
- **El lunes pasado** - Last Monday

### Question Words for Past
- **¿Qué hiciste?** - What did you do?
- **¿Adónde fuiste?** - Where did you go?

- **¿Con quién saliste?** - Who did you go out with?
- **¿Cuánto tiempo...?** - How long...?

## Conversation Scenarios

The AI tutor will guide you through conversations about:

### Describing Yesterday's Activities

Practice talking about your day yesterday using regular preterite verbs. Focus on sequencing events and using time expressions.

Topics to cover:
- "Ayer trabajé ocho horas" (Yesterday I worked eight hours)
- "Por la mañana desayuné cereal" (In the morning I ate cereal)
- "Después caminé al trabajo" (Then I walked to work)
- "Por la noche miré televisión" (At night I watched TV)

### Weekend Recap

Practice describing what you did last weekend, using multiple past tense verbs in sequence.

Topics to cover:
- "El sábado pasado salí con amigos" (Last Saturday I went out with friends)
- "Comimos en un restaurante italiano" (We ate at an Italian restaurant)
- "El domingo descansé en casa" (On Sunday I rested at home)
- "¿Qué hiciste tú?" (What did you do?)

### Asking About Past Experiences

Practice asking and answering questions about past activities using question words with preterite.

Topics to cover:
- "¿Dónde viviste antes?" (Where did you live before?)
- "¿Estudiaste español en la escuela?" (Did you study Spanish in school?)
- "¿Cuándo empezaste a trabajar aquí?" (When did you start working here?)
- "Viví en México hace tres años" (I lived in Mexico three years ago)

## Practice Patterns

- Describe your entire day yesterday using at least 6 different past tense verbs
- Tell a short story about last weekend using preterite tense
- Practice the spelling-change verbs: llegué, jugué, busqué, empecé
- Ask your tutor 5 questions about their past using "¿Qué...?", "¿Dónde...?", "¿Cuándo...?"
- Create sentences using different time expressions (ayer, anoche, la semana pasada)
- Practice all three conjugation patterns: -ar, -er, -ir verbs

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- Using present tense when you mean past: "Ayer como pizza" → "Ayer comí pizza"
- Forgetting accent marks on past tense: "Hable" → "Hablé" (accent changes meaning!)
- Wrong endings for -er/-ir verbs: "Comío" → "Comió"
- Mixing up tú/él forms: "Tú habló" → "Tú hablaste"
- Forgetting spelling changes: "Yo jugé" → "Yo jugué"
- Using "hace" incorrectly: "Hace en lunes" → "El lunes pasado" or "Hace dos días"
- Saying "en ayer" instead of just "ayer" (don't use "en" with ayer!)

## Cultural Notes

- In Spanish conversation, the preterite is used frequently for storytelling
- Past tense is essential for small talk about weekends and vacations
- "¿Qué tal el fin de semana?" (How was your weekend?) is a common Monday greeting
