## Grammar Focus

### Demonstrative Adjectives (This/That/These/Those)

**Rule:** Demonstratives must agree in gender and number with the noun they modify

| Distance | Masculine Singular | Feminine Singular | Masculine Plural | Feminine Plural |
|----------|-------------------|------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| Close (this/these) | este | esta | estos | estas |
| Medium (that/those) | ese | esa | esos | esas |
| Far (that/those) | aquel | aquella | aquellos | aquellas |

**Most common in shopping: este/esta/estos/estas**

Examples:
- Este vestido es bonito (This dress is pretty - masculine singular)
- Esta camisa es cara (This shirt is expensive - feminine singular)
- Estos zapatos son cómodos (These shoes are comfortable - masculine plural)
- Estas faldas son baratas (These skirts are cheap - feminine plural)

### Color Adjectives and Agreement

**Rule:** Color adjectives must agree in gender and number with the noun

Colors that change:
- Rojo/a, negro/a, blanco/a, amarillo/a (add -a for feminine, -s/-as for plural)
  - Un vestido rojo (red dress - masculine)
  - Una camisa roja (red shirt - feminine)
  - Unos pantalones rojos (red pants - masculine plural)

Colors that don't change:
- Azul, verde, gris, marrón (same for both genders, add -es for plural)
  - Un vestido azul (blue dress)
  - Una camisa azul (blue shirt)
  - Unos zapatos azules (blue shoes - plural)

### Numbers for Prices (Review)

0-10: cero, uno, dos, tres, cuatro, cinco, seis, siete, ocho, nueve, diez
11-15: once, doce, trece, catorce, quince
16-19: dieciséis, diecisiete, dieciocho, diecinueve
20-90: veinte, treinta, cuarenta, cincuenta, sesenta, setenta, ochenta, noventa
100: cien

Prices:
- 5 euros: cinco euros
- 15 euros: quince euros
- 25 euros: veinticinco euros
- 50 euros: cincuenta euros
- 100 euros: cien euros

### Asking Questions When Shopping

**¿Cuánto cuesta...?** - How much does... cost? (singular)
**¿Cuánto cuestan...?** - How much do... cost? (plural)

Examples:
- ¿Cuánto cuesta este vestido? (How much does this dress cost?)
- ¿Cuánto cuestan estos zapatos? (How much do these shoes cost?)

**¿Tiene...?** - Do you have...?
- ¿Tiene esto en azul? (Do you have this in blue?)
- ¿Tiene una talla más grande? (Do you have a larger size?)

## Practice Patterns

- Point to items and describe them: "Esta camisa es roja", "Estos zapatos son caros"
- Ask prices for 5 different items using ¿Cuánto cuesta/cuestan?
- Practice color agreement: una camisa roja, un vestido rojo, unos zapatos rojos
- Use demonstratives correctly: este/esta/estos/estas
- Role-play a complete shopping transaction from greeting to payment
- Practice saying "no" politely: "Es muy caro, gracias" (It's too expensive, thanks)

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- Wrong demonstrative gender: "Este falda" → "Esta falda" (falda is feminine)
- Wrong color agreement: "Una camisa rojo" → "Una camisa roja"
- Singular/plural confusion: "¿Cuánto cuestan este zapato?" → "¿Cuánto cuestan estos zapatos?"
- Forgetting plural article: "Quiero zapatos negros" (not "zapato negro" if you want both shoes!)
- Using wrong verb for cost: "¿Cuánto es?" (informal) vs. "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (more polite)
- Saying "en rojo" instead of just "rojo" when describing: "Una camisa roja" (not "en rojo")

## Cultural Notes

- In Spain and Latin America, clothing sizes are different from US/UK sizes
- Bargaining is common at markets (mercados) but NOT in regular stores
- Spanish shoe sizes are European sizes (EU 42 ≈ US 9)
- "Rebajas" (sales) happen twice a year: January-February and July-August
- It's polite to greet shopkeepers when entering: "Hola, buenos días"
- Many smaller shops close for siesta (2-5 PM) in Spain
