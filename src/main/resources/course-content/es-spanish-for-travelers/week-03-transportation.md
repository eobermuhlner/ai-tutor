## This Week's Goals

- Navigate public transportation systems
- Use taxi and rideshare services
- Purchase tickets and understand schedules
- Ask for directions and understand responses
- Handle transportation problems and delays

## Grammar Focus

### Expressing "I want to go to..."

**Rule:** Use "Quiero ir a..." (I want to go to...) to say where you want to go. This is your essential transportation phrase.

Examples:
- "Quiero ir al centro" (I want to go downtown)
- "Quiero ir a la estación" (I want to go to the station)
- "Quiero ir al aeropuerto" (I want to go to the airport)

### Asking About Transportation

**Rule:** Use these question patterns to get transportation information. Learn them as complete chunks.

Examples:
- "¿Qué autobús va a...?" (Which bus goes to...?)
- "¿Dónde está la parada?" (Where is the stop?)
- "¿Cuánto cuesta el boleto?" (How much is the ticket?)
- "¿A qué hora sale?" (What time does it leave?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Buying Bus or Metro Tickets

Practice purchasing tickets at a station, asking about fares, and understanding how to use transit cards or ticket machines.

Key phrases:
- "Un boleto para el centro, por favor" (One ticket to downtown, please)
- "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (How much does it cost?)
- "¿Dónde compro el boleto?" (Where do I buy the ticket?)
- "Quiero una tarjeta de transporte" (I want a transit card)
- "¿Cómo funciona?" (How does it work?)

### Taking a Bus or Metro

Practice asking which bus/metro goes to your destination, finding the right platform, and knowing when to get off.

Key phrases:
- "¿Qué autobús va al museo?" (Which bus goes to the museum?)
- "¿Este metro va a la estación central?" (Does this metro go to central station?)
- "¿Dónde bajo para el hotel?" (Where do I get off for the hotel?)
- "Avíseme cuando lleguemos, por favor" (Tell me when we arrive, please)
- "Quiero bajar en la próxima parada" (I want to get off at the next stop)

### Taking a Taxi

Practice hailing a taxi, telling the driver your destination, confirming the fare method, and paying.

Key phrases:
- "Al aeropuerto, por favor" (To the airport, please)
- "A esta dirección" (To this address) - show address on phone
- "¿Cuánto cuesta ir al centro?" (How much does it cost to go downtown?)
- "¿Usa taxímetro?" (Do you use a meter?)
- "¿Puedo pagar con tarjeta?" (Can I pay with card?)
- "Pare aquí, por favor" (Stop here, please)

### Asking for Directions to Transportation

Practice asking locals or information staff how to get to bus stops, metro stations, or train stations.

Key phrases:
- "¿Dónde está la parada de autobús?" (Where is the bus stop?)
- "¿Cómo llego a la estación de tren?" (How do I get to the train station?)
- "¿Está cerca?" (Is it close?)
- "¿Puedo ir caminando?" (Can I walk there?)
- "¿Cuánto tiempo se tarda?" (How long does it take?)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully purchase the correct ticket for your destination
- Navigate to the right bus/metro line and platform
- Ask when to get off and reach your destination
- Take a taxi and communicate your destination clearly
- Handle a missed bus/train and find the next one

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Quiero a ir a..." → "Quiero ir a..." (no "a" before "ir")
- "¿Qué autobús para el centro?" → "¿Qué autobús va al centro?" (need verb "va")
- "¿Dónde yo bajo?" → "¿Dónde bajo?" (no subject pronoun needed)
- "¿Cuánto es cuesta?" → "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (no "es" needed)
- "El metro va a la playa?" → "¿El metro va a la playa?" (need question marks!)
- "¿Puedo pagar en tarjeta?" → "¿Puedo pagar con tarjeta?" (use "con" not "en")
- Forgetting to say "por favor" when asking driver to stop → "Pare aquí, por favor" (politeness!)
- "Está mucho lejos" → "Está muy lejos" (use "muy" not "mucho" with adjectives)

## Cultural Notes

- **Metro/bus etiquette**: Let passengers exit before boarding. Offer seats to elderly, pregnant women, and disabled passengers. This is strongly expected.
- **Transit cards**: Most cities have rechargeable transit cards that are cheaper than single tickets. Ask at ticket window: "¿Tienen tarjeta de transporte?"
- **Ticket validation**: In some cities, you must validate tickets in machines before boarding or when entering metro. Fines for unvalidated tickets are heavy.
- **Bus payment**: In Latin America, often pay driver when boarding. In Spain, usually validate pre-purchased ticket. Ask locals if unsure.
- **Taxi meters**: In major cities, taxis use meters ("taxímetro"). In smaller towns or airports, negotiate price before starting. Agree clearly to avoid disputes.
- **Uber/Cabify**: Availability varies. Major cities have rideshare apps. Rural areas rely on traditional taxis. Download local rideshare app before arriving.
- **Colectivos**: In Argentina/Uruguay, "colectivos" are shared buses/vans. Pay driver, tell them your destination. They stop anywhere along route.
- **Schedules**: Public transit schedules are generally reliable in major cities. Smaller towns have less frequent service. Always check last departure time.
- **Safety**: Public transportation is generally safe. Watch belongings, especially in crowded buses/metros. Keep valuables close and zipped.
- **Rush hour**: Avoid traveling during rush hour (7-9am, 5-7pm) if possible. Transportation is extremely crowded and slow.
