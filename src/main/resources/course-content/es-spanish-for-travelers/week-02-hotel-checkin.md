## This Week's Goals

- Complete hotel check-in and check-out procedures
- Request specific room types and amenities
- Ask about hotel services and facilities
- Report room problems and request solutions
- Understand hotel policies and schedules

## Grammar Focus

### Expressing "I have" and "I need"

**Rule:** Use "Tengo..." (I have...) for things you have and "Necesito..." (I need...) for things you need. These are essential hotel survival phrases.

Examples:
- "Tengo una reserva" (I have a reservation)
- "Tengo una reserva a nombre de..." (I have a reservation under the name of...)
- "Necesito una habitación" (I need a room)
- "Necesito más toallas" (I need more towels)

### Asking About Availability and Services

**Rule:** Use "¿Tiene...?" (Do you have...?) and "¿Hay...?" (Is there...?) to ask about availability. Use "¿Dónde está...?" (Where is...?) for locations.

Examples:
- "¿Tiene habitaciones disponibles?" (Do you have rooms available?)
- "¿Hay WiFi?" (Is there WiFi?)
- "¿Hay desayuno incluido?" (Is breakfast included?)
- "¿Dónde está el gimnasio?" (Where is the gym?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In at the Hotel

Practice arriving at the hotel, confirming your reservation, providing identification, and getting your room key.

Key phrases:
- "Tengo una reserva a nombre de [Name]" (I have a reservation under [Name])
- "Aquí está mi pasaporte" (Here is my passport)
- "¿A qué hora es el desayuno?" (What time is breakfast?)
- "¿Dónde está mi habitación?" (Where is my room?)
- "¿Cuál es la contraseña del WiFi?" (What's the WiFi password?)

### Requesting Room Services and Amenities

Practice asking about hotel facilities, requesting extra items, and ordering room service.

Key phrases:
- "¿Dónde está la piscina?" (Where is the pool?)
- "Necesito más toallas" (I need more towels)
- "¿Pueden traer más almohadas?" (Can you bring more pillows?)
- "¿Tienen servicio a la habitación?" (Do you have room service?)
- "¿A qué hora cierra el gimnasio?" (What time does the gym close?)

### Reporting Room Problems

Practice reporting issues with your room (broken AC, no hot water, noise) and requesting solutions or room changes.

Key phrases:
- "El aire acondicionado no funciona" (The air conditioning doesn't work)
- "No hay agua caliente" (There's no hot water)
- "La habitación está muy sucia" (The room is very dirty)
- "Es muy ruidoso, no puedo dormir" (It's very noisy, I can't sleep)
- "¿Pueden cambiarme de habitación?" (Can you change my room?)
- "¿Cuándo pueden arreglarlo?" (When can you fix it?)

### Checking Out and Extending Stay

Practice reviewing your bill, checking out, arranging late checkout, or extending your stay.

Key phrases:
- "Quiero hacer el check-out" (I want to check out)
- "¿Puedo ver la cuenta?" (Can I see the bill?)
- "¿Qué es este cargo?" (What is this charge?)
- "¿Puedo dejar las maletas aquí?" (Can I leave my suitcases here?)
- "Quiero quedarme una noche más" (I want to stay one more night)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in and get to your room with WiFi password
- Request additional room items or services
- Report a room problem and negotiate a solution or room change
- Extend your stay by one or more nights
- Check out, review bill, and arrange luggage storage

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Tengo reserva" → "Tengo una reserva" (need article "una")
- "Vista a la mar" → "Vista al mar" (masculine article for "el mar")
- "¿Tiene disponible?" → "¿Tiene disponibilidad?" or "¿Tiene habitaciones disponibles?" (complete the phrase)
- "Necesito dos toalla" → "Necesito dos toallas" (plural)
- "El WiFi no camina" → "El WiFi no funciona" (use "funcionar" not "caminar" for technology)
- "Hace mucho ruido" when complaining → "Es muy ruidoso" or "Hay mucho ruido" (better phrasing)
- "¿Cuánto cuesta la noche?" → "¿Cuánto cuesta por noche?" (use "por noche")
- Forgetting "por favor" when requesting items → Always add "por favor" (basic politeness)

## Cultural Notes

- **Reservation confirmation**: Always carry printed or digital confirmation. Showing "Tengo una reserva" with email backup prevents issues.
- **Passport required**: Hotels legally must photocopy your passport in most Spanish-speaking countries. This is normal procedure.
- **Check-in/check-out times**: Check-in usually 2-3pm, check-out 11am-12pm. Early check-in may cost extra. Late check-out depends on availability.
- **Breakfast times**: Hotel breakfast often 7-10am. In Spain, may start later (8-11am). Arrive early if you need to leave for tours.
- **Room cleaning**: Housekeeping typically cleans before 2pm. Hang "No molestar" (Do not disturb) sign if sleeping late.
- **WiFi**: Most hotels have WiFi, but quality varies. Ask for password at check-in: "¿Cuál es la contraseña del WiFi?"
- **Deposits**: Some hotels require cash deposit or credit card authorization. Ensure you understand charges before checking in.
- **Tipping**: Not mandatory but appreciated. Leave 1-2 euros/dollars per night for housekeeping. Bellhops: 1-2 euros/dollars per bag.
- **Safe usage**: Use room safe for passport and valuables. Hotel generally not liable for items left in room.
- **Minibar charges**: Minibar items are expensive. Check prices before consuming. Sometimes cheaper to buy at nearby store.
- **Noise complaints**: If room is noisy, request room change immediately, not at checkout. Hotels can usually accommodate.
- **Problem resolution**: Report problems immediately, not at checkout. Hotels can only fix problems they know about.
