## This Week's Goals

- Understand restaurant menus and order food
- Express dietary preferences and allergies
- Ask about ingredients and preparation
- Request the bill and handle payment
- Navigate different types of eating establishments

## Grammar Focus

### Making Polite Requests

**Rule:** Use "Quiero..." (I want...) or "Para mí..." (For me...) to order. These are simple and effective for travelers.

Examples:
- "Quiero el pollo" (I want the chicken)
- "Para mí, la ensalada" (For me, the salad)
- "Quiero un café, por favor" (I want a coffee, please)

### Pointing at Menu Items

**Rule:** When unsure of pronunciation, use "Este" (this - masculine) or "Esta" (this - feminine) and point at the menu.

Examples:
- "Este, por favor" (This one, please - pointing at menu)
- "Quiero este plato" (I want this dish)
- "¿Qué es esto?" (What is this?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Arriving and Being Seated

Practice arriving at a restaurant, requesting a table, and being shown to your seat.

Key phrases:
- "Mesa para dos, por favor" (Table for two, please)
- "¿Tiene mesa disponible?" (Do you have a table available?)
- "Tengo una reserva a nombre de..." (I have a reservation under...)
- "¿Puedo sentarme afuera?" (Can I sit outside?)
- "¿Hay mesa cerca de la ventana?" (Is there a table near the window?)

### Ordering Food and Drinks

Practice looking at the menu, asking questions about dishes, and placing your order.

Key phrases:
- "¿Qué recomienda?" (What do you recommend?)
- "Quiero el menú del día" (I want the daily set menu)
- "Para mí, el pollo con arroz" (For me, the chicken with rice)
- "¿Qué lleva este plato?" (What's in this dish?)
- "Este, por favor" (This one, please - point at menu)
- "Para tomar, agua sin gas" (To drink, still water)

### Communicating Dietary Restrictions

Practice explaining allergies and dietary restrictions to ensure safe eating.

Key phrases:
- "Soy vegetariano, ¿qué me recomienda?" (I'm vegetarian, what do you recommend?)
- "Soy alérgico a los mariscos" (I'm allergic to seafood)
- "¿Este plato lleva maní?" (Does this dish have peanuts?)
- "Sin gluten, por favor" (Gluten-free, please)
- "¿Puede hacerlo sin carne?" (Can you make it without meat?)

### Requesting the Bill and Paying

Practice asking for the check, reviewing the bill, and completing payment.

Key phrases:
- "La cuenta, por favor" (The bill, please)
- "¿Cuánto es en total?" (How much is it total?)
- "¿Puedo pagar con tarjeta?" (Can I pay with card?)
- "¿Está incluida la propina?" (Is the tip included?)
- "Cuentas separadas, por favor" (Separate bills, please)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully order a complete meal (appetizer, main course, drink, dessert)
- Communicate a dietary restriction and get appropriate recommendations
- Handle a situation where your order is incorrect
- Request the bill and pay using your preferred method
- Navigate a full restaurant experience from arrival to payment

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Quiero un agua" → "Quiero agua" (no article needed with uncountable water)
- "Soy alérgico de maní" → "Soy alérgico al maní" (use "a/al" not "de")
- "¿Tiene vegetariano?" → "¿Tiene opciones vegetarianas?" (complete phrase)
- "Es mucho picante" → "Es muy picante" (use "muy" not "mucho" with adjectives)
- "La cuenta" without "por favor" → "La cuenta, por favor" (always be polite!)
- "¿Cuánto cuesta?" for bill → "¿Cuánto es?" (more natural for total bill)
- "Está incluido la propina?" → "¿Está incluida la propina?" (feminine agreement)
- Forgetting to say "está delicioso" → Complimenting food is appreciated culturally!

## Cultural Notes

- **Meal times**: In Spain and parts of Latin America, lunch is 2-4pm (main meal), dinner 9-11pm. Restaurants may be closed or empty at 6pm.
- **Menu del día**: In Spain, "menú del día" is a fixed-price lunch menu (usually 10-15 euros) with multiple courses. Excellent value.
- **Bread and water**: In some countries, bread is complimentary. In others, it's charged separately. Water is rarely free - you'll pay for bottled water.
- **Service pace**: Restaurant service is slower and more relaxed than in the US. Meals are leisurely experiences. Don't expect fast turnover.
- **Getting the bill**: Servers won't bring the bill until you ask. Waiting for you to finish is respectful. Say "La cuenta, por favor" when ready.
- **Tipping customs**: Spain: 5-10% optional, round up bill. Latin America: 10% standard. Check if "servicio" is included on bill.
- **Sharing dishes**: In many Spanish-speaking cultures, sharing plates ("tapas" in Spain) is common and encouraged.
- **"Buen provecho"**: Say this to others when they're eating (like "enjoy your meal"). People may say it to you when passing your table.
- **Dietary restrictions**: Always ask about ingredients. Cross-contamination awareness varies. Be clear and specific about allergies.
- **Reservations**: Popular restaurants require reservations, especially for dinner in Spain. Call ahead or ask hotel concierge.
