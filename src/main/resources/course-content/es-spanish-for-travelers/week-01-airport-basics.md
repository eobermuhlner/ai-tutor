## This Week's Goals

- Navigate airport check-in and boarding procedures

## Grammar Focus

### Asking Questions at the Airport

**Rule:** Use "¿Dónde está...?" (Where is...?) for locations and "¿A qué hora...?" (At what time...?) for schedules. These are your survival questions.

Examples:
- "¿Dónde está la puerta de embarque?" (Where is the boarding gate?)
- "¿A qué hora sale el vuelo?" (At what time does the flight leave?)
- "¿Dónde están los baños?" (Where are the bathrooms?)

### Essential Survival Phrases

**Rule:** Learn these complete phrases as chunks. Don't worry about grammar - just use them exactly as shown.

Examples:
- "No hablo español" (I don't speak Spanish)
- "¿Habla inglés?" (Do you speak English?)
- "No entiendo" (I don't understand)
- "Más despacio, por favor" (Slower, please)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In for Your Flight

Practice checking in at the airline counter, answering basic questions about luggage, and getting your boarding pass.

Key phrases:
- "Quiero hacer check-in" (I want to check in)
- "Aquí está mi pasaporte" (Here is my passport)
- "¿Puedo facturar una maleta?" (Can I check one suitcase?)
- "Prefiero asiento de ventanilla" (I prefer a window seat)
- "¿De qué puerta sale?" (Which gate does it leave from?)

### Going Through Security

Practice communicating at security checkpoint, understanding instructions about liquids and electronics, and asking for clarification.

Key phrases:
- "¿Dónde está el control de seguridad?" (Where is security?)
- "¿Puedo llevar esto?" (Can I bring this?)
- "No entiendo" (I don't understand)
- "¿Puede repetir, por favor?" (Can you repeat, please?)

### Finding Your Gate and Boarding

Practice locating your gate, asking about boarding time, and understanding boarding announcements.

Key phrases:
- "¿Dónde está la puerta 15?" (Where is gate 15?)
- "¿A qué hora embarcamos?" (What time do we board?)
- "¿Está retrasado el vuelo?" (Is the flight delayed?)
- "¿Dónde están los baños?" (Where are the bathrooms?)

### Immigration and Customs

Practice answering basic questions at immigration (purpose of visit, length of stay) and declaring items at customs.

Key phrases:
- "Turismo" (Tourism)
- "Una semana" (One week)
- "Dos semanas" (Two weeks)
- "No tengo nada que declarar" (I have nothing to declare)
- "¿Dónde está la salida?" (Where is the exit?)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in for a flight and get boarding pass
- Navigate to your gate using only Spanish directions
- Handle a delayed flight situation and ask for information
- Go through immigration and answer basic questions
- Find baggage claim and report a missing suitcase

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "¿Dónde es la puerta?" → "¿Dónde está la puerta?" (use "está" for locations, critical for finding gates!)
- "Hablo no español" → "No hablo español" (word order - "no" before verb)
- "Mi vuelo tiene retraso" → "Mi vuelo está retrasado" (more natural phrasing)
- "¿Puedo llevar esto?" spoken too quietly → Speak clearly at security (they need to hear you!)
- "Pasaporte, por favor" without "disculpe" first → "Disculpe, ¿mi pasaporte?" (be polite even when stressed)
- "Equipaje de la mano" → "Equipaje de mano" (no article needed)
- Forgetting to say "gracias" to staff → Always say "gracias" (basic politeness reduces stress)

## Cultural Notes

- **Arrive early**: Spanish-speaking countries' airports can have long lines at check-in and immigration. Arrive 3 hours early for international flights.
- **Formal greetings matter**: Always greet staff with "Buenos días" or "Buenas tardes" before making requests. This is more important than in some cultures.
- **"Disculpe" is essential**: Start every question or request with "Disculpe" (excuse me). Not doing so seems rude and gets worse service.
- **Immigration questions**: Be prepared to answer "¿Motivo del viaje?" (reason for trip) and "¿Cuánto tiempo se queda?" (how long are you staying). Simple answers: "Turismo, una semana" work fine.
- **Baggage weight limits**: Limits are strictly enforced. Budget airlines especially. Know the weight limit before arriving.
- **Speaking English**: Major international airports have English-speaking staff, but attempting Spanish first shows respect and gets better service.
- **Boarding process**: Boarding may be less organized than you're used to. Groups may board together. Listen for your row number or zone.
- **Security is strict**: Liquid limits (100ml containers), no sharp objects, electronics out of bags. Rules are enforced firmly - don't argue.
- **Documents accessible**: Keep passport and boarding pass easily accessible. You'll show them multiple times (check-in, security, gate, boarding).
- **Airport announcements**: Often in Spanish only at smaller airports. Watch screens carefully and ask staff if unsure.
