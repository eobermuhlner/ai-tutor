## This Week's Goals

- Complete hotel check-in and check-out procedures
- Request specific room types and amenities
- Ask about hotel services and facilities
- Report room problems and request solutions
- Understand hotel policies and schedules

## Grammar Focus

### Expressing "I have" and "I need"

**Rule:** Use "J'ai..." (I have...) for things you have and "J'ai besoin de..." (I need...) for things you need. These are essential hotel survival phrases.

Examples:
- "J'ai une réservation" (I have a reservation)
- "J'ai une réservation au nom de..." (I have a reservation under the name of...)
- "J'ai besoin d'une chambre" (I need a room)
- "J'ai besoin de plus de serviettes" (I need more towels)

### Asking About Availability and Services

**Rule:** Use "Avez-vous...?" (Do you have...?) and "Y a-t-il...?" (Is there...?) to ask about availability. Use "Où est...?" (Where is...?) for locations.

Examples:
- "Avez-vous des chambres disponibles?" (Do you have rooms available?)
- "Y a-t-il le WiFi?" (Is there WiFi?)
- "Le petit-déjeuner est inclus?" (Is breakfast included?)
- "Où est la salle de sport?" (Where is the gym?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In at the Hotel

Practice arriving at the hotel, confirming your reservation, providing identification, and getting your room key.

Key phrases:
- "J'ai une réservation au nom de [Name]" (I have a reservation under [Name])
- "Voici mon passeport" (Here is my passport)
- "À quelle heure est le petit-déjeuner?" (What time is breakfast?)
- "Où est ma chambre?" (Where is my room?)
- "Quel est le mot de passe WiFi?" (What's the WiFi password?)

### Requesting Room Services and Amenities

Practice asking about hotel facilities, requesting extra items, and ordering room service.

Key phrases:
- "Où est la piscine?" (Where is the pool?)
- "J'ai besoin de plus de serviettes" (I need more towels)
- "Pouvez-vous apporter plus d'oreillers?" (Can you bring more pillows?)
- "Avez-vous le service en chambre?" (Do you have room service?)
- "À quelle heure ferme la salle de sport?" (What time does the gym close?)

### Reporting Room Problems

Practice reporting issues with your room (broken AC, no hot water, noise) and requesting solutions or room changes.

Key phrases:
- "La climatisation ne marche pas" (The air conditioning doesn't work)
- "Il n'y a pas d'eau chaude" (There's no hot water)
- "La chambre est très sale" (The room is very dirty)
- "C'est très bruyant, je ne peux pas dormir" (It's very noisy, I can't sleep)
- "Pouvez-vous me changer de chambre?" (Can you change my room?)
- "Quand pouvez-vous le réparer?" (When can you fix it?)

### Checking Out and Extending Stay

Practice reviewing your bill, checking out, arranging late checkout, or extending your stay.

Key phrases:
- "Je voudrais régler" (I would like to check out)
- "Puis-je voir la note?" (Can I see the bill?)
- "Qu'est-ce que c'est, ce frais?" (What is this charge?)
- "Puis-je laisser mes valises ici?" (Can I leave my suitcases here?)
- "Je voudrais rester une nuit de plus" (I want to stay one more night)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in and get to your room with WiFi password
- Request additional room items or services
- Report a room problem and negotiate a solution or room change
- Extend your stay by one or more nights
- Check out, review bill, and arrange luggage storage

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "J'ai reserva" → "J'ai une réservation" (need article "une")
- "Vue sur le mer" → "Vue sur la mer" (feminine article for "la mer")
- "Avez-vous disponible?" → "Avez-vous de la disponibilité?" or "Avez-vous des chambres disponibles?" (complete the phrase)
- "J'ai besoin deux serviettes" → "J'ai besoin de deux serviettes" (need "de" before object)
- "Le WiFi ne marche" → "Le WiFi ne marche pas" (need "pas" for negation)
- "Il y a beaucoup de bruit" when complaining → "C'est très bruyant" or "Il y a beaucoup de bruit" (both work)
- "Combien coûte la nuit?" → "Combien par nuit?" (more natural)
- Forgetting "s'il vous plaît" when requesting items → Always add "s'il vous plaît" (basic politeness)

## Cultural Notes

- **Reservation confirmation**: Always carry printed or digital confirmation. Showing "J'ai une réservation" with email backup prevents issues.
- **Passport required**: Hotels legally must record your passport information in France. This is normal procedure.
- **Check-in/check-out times**: Check-in usually 2-3pm, check-out 11am-12pm. Early check-in may cost extra. Late check-out depends on availability.
- **Breakfast times**: Hotel breakfast often 7-10am. In France, may include croissants, baguettes, jam, and coffee - typically continental style.
- **Room cleaning**: Housekeeping typically cleans before 2pm. Hang "Ne pas déranger" (Do not disturb) sign if sleeping late.
- **WiFi**: Most hotels have WiFi, but quality varies. Ask for password at check-in: "Quel est le mot de passe WiFi?"
- **Deposits**: Some hotels require cash deposit or credit card authorization. Ensure you understand charges before checking in.
- **Tipping**: Not mandatory in France (service included), but appreciated. Leave 1-2 euros per night for housekeeping. Bellhops: 1-2 euros per bag.
- **Safe usage**: Use room safe for passport and valuables. Hotel generally not liable for items left in room.
- **Minibar charges**: Minibar items are expensive. Check prices before consuming. Often cheaper to buy at nearby store.
- **Noise complaints**: If room is noisy, request room change immediately, not at checkout. Hotels can usually accommodate.
- **Problem resolution**: Report problems immediately ("tout de suite"), not at checkout. Hotels can only fix problems they know about.
- **Formal language**: Use "vous" form (formal) with all hotel staff. "Tu" form is only for friends and family.
