## This Week's Goals

- Navigate airport check-in and boarding procedures

## Grammar Focus

### Asking Questions at the Airport

**Rule:** Use "Où est...?" (Where is...?) for locations and "À quelle heure...?" (At what time...?) for schedules. These are your survival questions.

Examples:
- "Où est la porte d'embarquement?" (Where is the boarding gate?)
- "À quelle heure part le vol?" (At what time does the flight leave?)
- "Où sont les toilettes?" (Where are the bathrooms?)

### Essential Survival Phrases

**Rule:** Learn these complete phrases as chunks. Don't worry about grammar - just use them exactly as shown.

Examples:
- "Je ne parle pas français" (I don't speak French)
- "Parlez-vous anglais?" (Do you speak English?)
- "Je ne comprends pas" (I don't understand)
- "Plus lentement, s'il vous plaît" (Slower, please)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In for Your Flight

Practice checking in at the airline counter, answering basic questions about luggage, and getting your boarding pass.

Key phrases:
- "Je voudrais m'enregistrer" (I would like to check in)
- "Voici mon passeport" (Here is my passport)
- "Puis-je enregistrer une valise?" (Can I check one suitcase?)
- "Je préfère un hublot" (I prefer a window seat)
- "De quelle porte part-il?" (Which gate does it leave from?)

### Going Through Security

Practice communicating at security checkpoint, understanding instructions about liquids and electronics, and asking for clarification.

Key phrases:
- "Où est le contrôle de sécurité?" (Where is security?)
- "Puis-je emporter ceci?" (Can I bring this?)
- "Je ne comprends pas" (I don't understand)
- "Pouvez-vous répéter, s'il vous plaît?" (Can you repeat, please?)

### Finding Your Gate and Boarding

Practice locating your gate, asking about boarding time, and understanding boarding announcements.

Key phrases:
- "Où est la porte 15?" (Where is gate 15?)
- "À quelle heure embarquons-nous?" (What time do we board?)
- "Le vol est-il en retard?" (Is the flight delayed?)
- "Où sont les toilettes?" (Where are the bathrooms?)

### Immigration and Customs

Practice answering basic questions at immigration (purpose of visit, length of stay) and declaring items at customs.

Key phrases:
- "Tourisme" (Tourism)
- "Une semaine" (One week)
- "Deux semaines" (Two weeks)
- "Je n'ai rien à déclarer" (I have nothing to declare)
- "Où est la sortie?" (Where is the exit?)

## Practice Patterns

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Où es la porte?" → "Où est la porte?" (correct conjugation of "être")
- "Je parle pas français" → "Je ne parle pas français" (need both "ne" and "pas")
- "Mon vol a retard" → "Mon vol est en retard" (use "être" not "avoir")
- "Puis-je emporter ceci?" spoken too quietly → Speak clearly at security (they need to hear you!)
- "Passeport, s'il vous plaît" without "Excusez-moi" first → "Excusez-moi, mon passeport?" (be polite even when stressed)
- "Bagage à la main" → "Bagage à main" (no article needed)
- Forgetting to say "merci" to staff → Always say "merci" (basic politeness reduces stress)

## Cultural Notes

- **Arrive early**: French airports can have long lines at check-in and immigration. Arrive 3 hours early for international flights, especially at Paris CDG.
- **Formal greetings matter**: Always greet staff with "Bonjour" or "Bonsoir" before making requests. Not greeting first is considered rude in French culture.
- **"S'il vous plaît" is essential**: Use formal "vous" form with airport staff. "S'il te plaît" (informal) is only for friends and children.
- **Immigration questions**: Be prepared to answer "Motif du voyage?" (reason for trip) and "Combien de temps restez-vous?" (how long are you staying). Simple answers work fine.
- **Baggage weight limits**: Limits are strictly enforced. Budget airlines especially. Know the weight limit before arriving.
- **Speaking English**: Major French airports have some English-speaking staff, but attempting French first shows respect and gets significantly better service.
- **Boarding process**: French boarding is usually organized by zones or seat rows. Listen for your zone number.
- **Security is strict**: Liquid limits (100ml containers), no sharp objects, electronics out of bags. Rules are enforced firmly.
- **Documents accessible**: Keep passport and boarding pass easily accessible. You'll show them multiple times.
- **Airport announcements**: Often in French first, then English. Watch screens carefully if you don't understand French announcements.
- **"Excusez-moi" vs "Pardon"**: "Excusez-moi" to get attention, "Pardon" after bumping into someone. Both are polite.
