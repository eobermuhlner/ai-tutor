## This Week's Goals

- Complete hotel check-in and check-out procedures
- Request specific room types and amenities
- Ask about hotel services and facilities
- Report room problems and request solutions
- Understand hotel policies and schedules

## Grammar Focus

### Expressing "I have" and "I need"

**Rule:** Use "Ho..." (I have...) for things you have and "Ho bisogno di..." (I need...) for things you need. These are essential hotel survival phrases.

Examples:
- "Ho una prenotazione" (I have a reservation)
- "Ho una prenotazione a nome di..." (I have a reservation under the name...)
- "Ho bisogno di una camera" (I need a room)
- "Ho bisogno di più asciugamani" (I need more towels)

### Asking About Availability and Services

**Rule:** Use "Avete...?" (Do you have...?) and "C'è...?" (Is there...?) to ask about availability. Use "Dov'è...?" (Where is...?) for locations.

Examples:
- "Avete camere libere?" (Do you have rooms available?)
- "C'è il WiFi?" (Is there WiFi?)
- "La colazione è inclusa?" (Is breakfast included?)
- "Dov'è la palestra?" (Where is the gym?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In at the Hotel

Practice arriving at the hotel, confirming your reservation, providing identification, and getting your room key.

Key phrases:
- "Ho una prenotazione a nome di [Name]" (I have a reservation under [Name])
- "Ecco il mio passaporto" (Here is my passport)
- "A che ora viene servita la colazione?" (What time is breakfast?)
- "Dov'è la mia camera?" (Where is my room?)
- "Qual è la password del WiFi?" (What's the WiFi password?)

### Requesting Room Services and Amenities

Practice asking about hotel facilities, requesting extra items, and ordering room service.

Key phrases:
- "Dov'è la piscina?" (Where is the pool?)
- "Ho bisogno di più asciugamani" (I need more towels)
- "Può portare più cuscini?" (Can you bring more pillows?)
- "Avete servizio in camera?" (Do you have room service?)
- "A che ora chiude la palestra?" (What time does the gym close?)

### Reporting Room Problems

Practice reporting issues with your room (broken AC, no hot water, noise) and requesting solutions or room changes.

Key phrases:
- "L'aria condizionata non funziona" (The air conditioning doesn't work)
- "Non c'è acqua calda" (There's no hot water)
- "La camera è molto sporca" (The room is very dirty)
- "È molto rumoroso, non riesco a dormire" (It's very noisy, I can't sleep)
- "Può darmi un'altra camera?" (Can you give me another room?)
- "Quando può aggiustarlo?" (When can you fix it?)

### Checking Out and Extending Stay

Practice reviewing your bill, checking out, arranging late checkout, or extending your stay.

Key phrases:
- "Vorrei fare il check-out" (I would like to check out)
- "Posso vedere il conto?" (Can I see the bill?)
- "Cos'è questa spesa?" (What is this charge?)
- "Posso lasciare le valigie qui?" (Can I leave my suitcases here?)
- "Vorrei rimanere un'altra notte" (I want to stay one more night)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in and get to your room with WiFi password
- Request additional room items or services
- Report a room problem and negotiate a solution or room change
- Extend your stay by one or more nights
- Check out, review bill, and arrange luggage storage

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Ho prenotazione" → "Ho una prenotazione" (need article "una")
- "Vista sul mare" → "Vista mare" (Italian uses compound noun without "sul")
- "Avete disponibile?" → "Avete camere libere?" or "Ci sono camere?" (complete the phrase)
- "Ho bisogno due asciugamani" → "Ho bisogno di due asciugamani" (need "di")
- "Il WiFi non va" → "Il WiFi non funziona" (use "funzionare" for technology)
- "C'è molto rumore" when complaining → "È molto rumoroso" (more natural)
- "Quanto costa la notte?" → "Quanto costa per notte?" (use "per notte")
- Forgetting "per favore" when requesting items → Always add "per favore" (basic politeness)

## Cultural Notes

- **Reservation confirmation**: Always carry printed or digital confirmation. Italians expect documentation, especially in tourist areas.
- **Passport required**: Hotels legally must record your passport information in Italy. This is normal procedure for all hotels.
- **Check-in/check-out times**: Check-in usually 2-3pm, check-out 10-11am. Early check-in may not be available. Late check-out depends on availability.
- **Breakfast times**: Hotel breakfast often 7-10am. Italian breakfast is lighter than American: coffee, pastries, bread, jam. Some hotels have buffets with cold cuts and cheese.
- **Room cleaning**: Housekeeping typically cleans before 2pm. Hang "Non disturbare" (Do not disturb) sign if sleeping late.
- **WiFi**: Most Italian hotels have WiFi, but quality can vary, especially in older buildings. Always ask for password: "Qual è la password del WiFi?"
- **Deposits**: Some hotels require cash deposit or credit card authorization. Ensure you understand charges before checking in.
- **Tipping**: Not mandatory in Italy but appreciated. Leave 1-2 euros per night for housekeeping. Bellhops: 1-2 euros per bag.
- **Safe usage**: Use room safe for passport and valuables. Hotels generally not liable for items left in room.
- **Minibar charges**: Minibar items are expensive. Check prices before consuming. Often cheaper to buy at nearby store.
- **Noise complaints**: If room is noisy, request room change immediately, not at checkout. Hotels can usually accommodate.
- **Problem resolution**: Report problems immediately, not at checkout. Italians appreciate directness - be clear about issues.
- **Formal language**: Use "lei" form (formal) with all hotel staff. "Tu" form is only for friends and family.
