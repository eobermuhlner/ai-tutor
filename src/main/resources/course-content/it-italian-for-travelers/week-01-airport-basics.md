## Grammar Focus

### Asking Questions at the Airport

**Rule:** Use "Dov'è...?" (Where is...?) for locations and "A che ora...?" (At what time...?) for schedules. These are your survival questions.

Examples:
- "Dov'è il gate?" (Where is the gate?)
- "A che ora parte l'aereo?" (At what time does the plane leave?)
- "Dove sono i bagni?" (Where are the bathrooms?)

### Essential Survival Phrases

**Rule:** Learn these complete phrases as chunks. Don't worry about grammar - just use them exactly as shown.

Examples:
- "Non parlo italiano" (I don't speak Italian)
- "Parla inglese?" (Do you speak English?)
- "Non capisco" (I don't understand)
- "Più lentamente, per favore" (Slower, please)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In for Your Flight

Practice checking in at the airline counter, answering basic questions about luggage, and getting your boarding pass.

Key phrases:
- "Vorrei fare il check-in" (I would like to check in)
- "Ecco il mio passaporto" (Here is my passport)
- "Posso registrare una valigia?" (Can I check one suitcase?)
- "Vorrei un posto al finestrino" (I would like a window seat)
- "Da quale gate parte?" (Which gate does it leave from?)

### Going Through Security

Practice communicating at security checkpoint, understanding instructions about liquids and electronics, and asking for clarification.

Key phrases:
- "Dov'è il controllo di sicurezza?" (Where is security?)
- "Posso portare questo?" (Can I bring this?)
- "Non capisco" (I don't understand)
- "Può ripetere, per favore?" (Can you repeat, please?)

### Finding Your Gate and Boarding

Practice locating your gate, asking about boarding time, and understanding boarding announcements.

Key phrases:
- "Dov'è il gate 15?" (Where is gate 15?)
- "A che ora si imbarca?" (What time do we board?)
- "Il volo è in ritardo?" (Is the flight delayed?)
- "Dove sono i bagni?" (Where are the bathrooms?)

### Immigration and Customs

Practice answering basic questions at immigration (purpose of visit, length of stay) and declaring items at customs.

Key phrases:
- "Turismo" (Tourism)
- "Una settimana" (One week)
- "Due settimane" (Two weeks)
- "Non ho niente da dichiarare" (I have nothing to declare)
- "Dov'è l'uscita?" (Where is the exit?)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in for a flight and get boarding pass
- Navigate to your gate using only Italian directions
- Handle a delayed flight situation and ask for information
- Go through immigration and answer basic questions

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Dove è il gate?" → "Dov'è il gate?" (use contracted form "dov'è")
- "Non parlo Italiano" → "Non parlo italiano" (don't capitalize "italiano" mid-sentence)
- "Il mio volo ha delay" → "Il mio volo è in ritardo" (use Italian word)
- "Posso portare questo?" spoken too quietly → Speak clearly at security (they need to hear you!)
- "Passaporto, per favore" without "Scusi" first → "Scusi, il mio passaporto?" (be polite even when stressed)
- "Bagaglio a mano" with wrong stress → "baga-GLI-o" (stress on -gli-)
- Forgetting to say "grazie" to staff → Always say "grazie" (basic politeness reduces stress)

## Cultural Notes

- **Arrive early**: Italian airports, especially Rome Fiumicino and Milan Malpensa, can be crowded. Arrive 2-3 hours early for international flights.
- **Formal greetings matter**: Always greet staff with "Buongiorno" (good morning) or "Buonasera" (good evening, after 2pm) before making requests. Italians value courtesy.
- **"Per favore" and "Grazie" are essential**: Use "per favore" (please) and "grazie" (thank you) constantly. Directness without these words seems very rude.
- **Immigration questions**: Be prepared to answer "Motivo del viaggio?" (purpose of trip) and "Quanto tempo rimane?" (how long are you staying). Keep answers brief and clear.
- **Baggage weight limits**: Weight limits are strictly enforced, especially by budget airlines like Ryanair and easyJet. Know the limit before arriving.
- **Speaking English**: Major Italian airports (Rome, Milan, Venice) have English-speaking staff, but attempting Italian first shows respect and gets better service.
- **Boarding process**: Italian boarding can be less orderly than in Northern Europe - people may crowd the gate. Stay calm and wait for your zone/row to be called.
- **Security is thorough**: Liquid limits (100ml containers), no sharp objects, electronics out of bags. Follow rules precisely.
- **Documents accessible**: Keep passport and boarding pass easily accessible. You'll show them multiple times (check-in, security, gate, boarding).
- **Strikes**: Italian airport and airline strikes ("sciopero") are relatively common. Check for strike notices before your travel day.
- **"Lei" vs "tu"**: Use "lei" (formal you) with all airport staff. "Tu" is only for friends and family.
