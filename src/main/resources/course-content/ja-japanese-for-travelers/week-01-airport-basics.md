## Grammar Focus

### Asking Questions at the Airport

**Rule:** Use "どこですか" (doko desu ka - Where is...?) for locations and "何時ですか" (nan-ji desu ka - What time is...?) for schedules. These are your survival questions.

Examples:
- "ゲートはどこですか？" (Gēto wa doko desu ka? - Where is the gate?)
- "何時に出発しますか？" (Nan-ji ni shuppatsu shimasu ka? - What time does it depart?)
- "トイレはどこですか？" (Toire wa doko desu ka? - Where is the bathroom?)

### Essential Survival Phrases

**Rule:** Learn these complete phrases as chunks. Don't worry about grammar - just use them exactly as shown. Pronunciation shown in parentheses.

Examples:
- "日本語が分かりません" (Nihongo ga wakarimasen - I don't understand Japanese)
- "英語が話せますか？" (Eigo ga hanasemasu ka? - Can you speak English?)
- "もう一度お願いします" (Mō ichi-do onegaishimasu - One more time please)
- "ゆっくりお願いします" (Yukkuri onegaishimasu - Slowly please)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In for Your Flight

Practice checking in at the airline counter, answering basic questions about luggage, and getting your boarding pass.

Key phrases:
- "チェックインしたいです" (Chekkuin shitai desu - I want to check in)
- "パスポートです" (Pasupōto desu - Here is my passport)
- "スーツケース一つを預けたいです" (Sūtsukēsu hitotsu wo azuketai desu - I want to check one suitcase)
- "窓側がいいです" (Mado-gawa ga ii desu - I prefer a window seat)
- "何番ゲートですか？" (Nan-ban gēto desu ka? - Which gate?)

### Going Through Security

Practice communicating at security checkpoint, understanding instructions about liquids and electronics, and asking for clarification.

Key phrases:
- "保安検査はどこですか？" (Hoan kensa wa doko desu ka? - Where is security?)
- "これを持っていけますか？" (Kore wo motte ikemasu ka? - Can I bring this?)
- "分かりません" (Wakarimasen - I don't understand)
- "もう一度お願いします" (Mō ichi-do onegaishimasu - Please repeat)

### Finding Your Gate and Boarding

Practice locating your gate, asking about boarding time, and understanding boarding announcements.

Key phrases:
- "15番ゲートはどこですか？" (Jū-go-ban gēto wa doko desu ka? - Where is gate 15?)
- "いつ搭乗しますか？" (Itsu tōjō shimasu ka? - What time do we board?)
- "フライトは遅れていますか？" (Furaito wa okurete imasu ka? - Is the flight delayed?)
- "トイレはどこですか？" (Toire wa doko desu ka? - Where are the bathrooms?)

### Immigration and Customs

Practice answering basic questions at immigration (purpose of visit, length of stay) and declaring items at customs.

Key phrases:
- "観光です" (Kankō desu - Tourism)
- "一週間です" (Isshūkan desu - One week)
- "二週間です" (Ni-shūkan desu - Two weeks)
- "申告するものはありません" (Shinkoku suru mono wa arimasen - I have nothing to declare)
- "出口はどこですか？" (Deguchi wa doko desu ka? - Where is the exit?)

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "ゲートはどこ？" → "ゲートはどこですか？" (add "desu ka" for politeness - essential in Japanese culture!)
- "分からない" → "分かりません" (use polite form "masen" not casual "nai")
- Speaking without "すみません" first → "すみません、..." (always say "excuse me" before questions)
- "英語話せる？" → "英語が話せますか？" (add particles "ga" and polite question "ka")
- Saying "はい" too softly → Speak clearly even if nervous (Japanese people are patient with learners)
- "ありがとう" → "ありがとうございます" (use full polite form "gozaimasu" with staff)
- Forgetting to bow slightly when saying thanks → Small bow shows respect

## Cultural Notes

- **Arrive very early**: Japanese airports are efficient but thorough. Arrive 3 hours early for international flights to avoid stress.
- **Politeness is critical**: Always use です/ます polite forms with staff. Casual forms are considered rude to strangers.
- **"すみません" starts everything**: Begin every question or request with "すみません" (excuse me). This is more important than in most cultures.
- **Bowing matters**: Small bow (15 degrees) when greeting staff, thanking, or apologizing. Japanese people notice and appreciate this.
- **Immigration questions**: Be prepared for "何の目的ですか？" (purpose of trip) and "どのくらい滞在しますか？" (how long staying). Simple answers: "観光、一週間" work fine.
- **Speaking English**: Major airports (Narita, Haneda, Kansai) have English-speaking staff, but attempting Japanese first shows respect and gets warmer service.
- **Baggage rules**: Weight and size limits are strictly enforced. Check airline rules before packing.
- **Security is efficient**: Follow instructions carefully. Remove electronics, liquids in clear bags. Process is fast if you're prepared.
- **Documents ready**: Keep passport and boarding pass accessible. You'll show them multiple times. Don't pack them deep in bags.
- **Quiet environment**: Japanese airports are quieter than Western airports. Speak softly, don't talk loudly on phones. Respect the atmosphere.
- **Announcements in Japanese first**: English announcements may come second or not at all at smaller airports. Watch screens carefully and ask staff if unsure.
- **Helpful staff**: Japanese airport staff are extremely helpful to tourists. Don't hesitate to ask for help - they expect it and are well-trained.
