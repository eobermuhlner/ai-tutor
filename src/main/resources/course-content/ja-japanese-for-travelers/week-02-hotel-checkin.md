## This Week's Goals

- Check in and out of hotels smoothly
- Request specific room types and amenities
- Ask about hotel facilities and services
- Report room problems and request assistance
- Handle payment and receipts

## Grammar Focus

### Making Requests and Preferences

**Rule:** Use "～がいいです" (~ ga ii desu - I prefer/would like...) for preferences and "～をお願いします" (~ wo onegaishimasu - please give me...) for requests.

Examples:
- "禁煙の部屋がいいです" (Kin-en no heya ga ii desu - I prefer a non-smoking room)
- "2泊お願いします" (Ni-haku onegaishimasu - Two nights please)
- "領収書をお願いします" (Ryōshū-sho wo onegaishimasu - Receipt please)

### Asking About Availability and Services

**Rule:** Use "～がありますか？" (~ ga arimasu ka? - Is there...? / Do you have...?) for asking about facilities and services.

Examples:
- "Wi-Fiがありますか？" (Wi-Fi ga arimasu ka? - Is there Wi-Fi?)
- "朝食は含まれていますか？" (Chōshoku wa fukumarete imasu ka? - Is breakfast included?)
- "空いている部屋がありますか？" (Aite iru heya ga arimasu ka? - Do you have available rooms?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In with a Reservation

Practice checking in at the hotel front desk with a confirmed reservation, providing your details, and getting your room key.

Key phrases:
- "予約しています" (Yoyaku shite imasu - I have a reservation)
- "名前は...です" (Namae wa ... desu - My name is...)
- "確認番号は...です" (Kakunin bangō wa ... desu - Confirmation number is...)
- "パスポートです" (Pasupōto desu - Here is my passport)
- "朝食は含まれていますか？" (Chōshoku wa fukumarete imasu ka? - Is breakfast included?)

### Requesting a Room Without Reservation

Practice asking about room availability, discussing room types and prices, and making a booking on the spot.

Key phrases:
- "空いている部屋がありますか？" (Aite iru heya ga arimasu ka? - Do you have available rooms?)
- "一泊いくらですか？" (Ippaku ikura desu ka? - How much is one night?)
- "禁煙の部屋がいいです" (Kin-en no heya ga ii desu - I prefer non-smoking room)
- "Wi-Fiがありますか？" (Wi-Fi ga arimasu ka? - Is there Wi-Fi?)

### Reporting Room Problems

Practice reporting issues with your room (broken AC, no hot water, noisy neighbors) and requesting assistance or a room change.

Key phrases:
- "問題があります" (Mondai ga arimasu - There is a problem)
- "エアコンが動きません" (Eakon ga ugokimasen - Air conditioning doesn't work)
- "お湯が出ません" (Oyu ga demasen - No hot water)
- "部屋を変えてください" (Heya wo kaete kudasai - Please change my room)

### Checking Out and Payment

Practice checking out, reviewing charges, requesting a receipt, and asking about luggage storage.

Key phrases:
- "チェックアウトお願いします" (Chekkuauto onegaishimasu - Check-out please)
- "クレジットカードで払います" (Kurejitto kādo de haraimasu - I'll pay by credit card)
- "領収書をお願いします" (Ryōshū-sho wo onegaishimasu - Receipt please)
- "荷物を預かってください" (Nimotsu wo azukatte kudasai - Please hold my luggage)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in with a reservation
- Negotiate room type and price without a reservation
- Report a room problem and get it resolved
- Ask about hotel facilities and breakfast times
- Check out and request a receipt and luggage storage

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "部屋がいい" → "部屋がいいです" (always add "desu" for politeness with hotel staff!)
- "Wi-Fiある？" → "Wi-Fiがありますか？" (use polite question form "arimasu ka" not casual "aru")
- "タオルちょうだい" → "タオルをお願いします" (use "onegaishimasu" not casual "chōdai")
- Speaking without "すみません" first → "すみません、..." (always say "excuse me" before requests)
- "朝食は何時？" → "朝食は何時ですか？" (add "desu ka" for polite questions)
- Not bowing when thanking staff → Small bow when receiving key or help shows respect
- Raising voice when there's a problem → Stay calm and polite - loud complaints are culturally inappropriate

## Cultural Notes

- **Remove shoes**: In traditional ryokan (Japanese inns), remove shoes at entrance and wear provided slippers. Never wear outdoor shoes on tatami mats.
- **Onsen/bath etiquette**: If hotel has onsen (hot spring bath), wash thoroughly before entering communal bath. Tattoos may not be allowed in some onsen.
- **Quiet hours**: Japanese hotels value quiet. Avoid loud talking, TV, or phone calls late at night. Thin walls mean neighbors hear everything.
- **Key return**: Some hotels require you to leave room key at front desk when going out. Ask "鍵を預けますか？" (Do I leave the key?).
- **Breakfast times**: Hotel breakfast typically 7-9 AM. Missing it means no refund. Arrive early if staying at busy hotel.
- **Slippers usage**: Wear provided slippers in room and hallways, but switch to toilet slippers inside bathroom (separate slippers at bathroom entrance).
- **Check-out time**: Usually 10-11 AM, strictly enforced. Late check-out (レイトチェックアウト) may cost extra. Ask when checking in.
- **Luggage storage**: Hotels will hold luggage after check-out free of charge. Say "荷物を預かってください" (Please hold my luggage).
- **Cash preference**: Some smaller hotels and ryokan prefer cash. Always ask "クレジットカードは使えますか？" (Can I use credit card?) when checking in.
- **No tipping**: Tipping is not customary in Japan and may be refused or cause confusion. Excellent service is expected standard.
- **Room size**: Japanese hotel rooms are typically smaller than Western hotels. Don't be surprised if room feels compact - this is normal.
- **Politeness pays**: Using polite Japanese (です/ます forms) and saying "ありがとうございます" gets you better service and warmer treatment.
