## This Week's Goals

- Navigate airport check-in and boarding procedures

## Grammar Focus

### Asking Questions at the Airport

**Rule:** Use "Wo ist...?" (Where is...?) for locations and "Um wie viel Uhr...?" (At what time...?) for schedules. These are your survival questions.

Examples:
- "Wo ist das Gate?" (Where is the gate?)
- "Um wie viel Uhr fliegt das Flugzeug?" (At what time does the plane fly?)
- "Wo sind die Toiletten?" (Where are the bathrooms?)

### Essential Survival Phrases

**Rule:** Learn these complete phrases as chunks. Don't worry about grammar - just use them exactly as shown.

Examples:
- "Ich spreche kein Deutsch" (I don't speak German)
- "Sprechen Sie Englisch?" (Do you speak English?)
- "Ich verstehe nicht" (I don't understand)
- "Langsamer, bitte" (Slower, please)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In for Your Flight

Practice checking in at the airline counter, answering basic questions about luggage, and getting your boarding pass.

Key phrases:
- "Ich möchte einchecken" (I would like to check in)
- "Hier ist mein Reisepass" (Here is my passport)
- "Kann ich einen Koffer aufgeben?" (Can I check one suitcase?)
- "Ich hätte gern einen Fensterplatz" (I would like a window seat)
- "Von welchem Gate geht es ab?" (Which gate does it leave from?)

### Going Through Security

Practice communicating at security checkpoint, understanding instructions about liquids and electronics, and asking for clarification.

Key phrases:
- "Wo ist die Sicherheitskontrolle?" (Where is security?)
- "Kann ich das mitnehmen?" (Can I bring this?)
- "Ich verstehe nicht" (I don't understand)
- "Können Sie das bitte wiederholen?" (Can you repeat that, please?)

### Finding Your Gate and Boarding

Practice locating your gate, asking about boarding time, and understanding boarding announcements.

Key phrases:
- "Wo ist Gate 15?" (Where is gate 15?)
- "Um wie viel Uhr steigen wir ein?" (What time do we board?)
- "Hat der Flug Verspätung?" (Is the flight delayed?)
- "Wo sind die Toiletten?" (Where are the bathrooms?)

### Immigration and Customs

Practice answering basic questions at immigration (purpose of visit, length of stay) and declaring items at customs.

Key phrases:
- "Tourismus" (Tourism)
- "Eine Woche" (One week)
- "Zwei Wochen" (Two weeks)
- "Ich habe nichts zu verzollen" (I have nothing to declare)
- "Wo ist der Ausgang?" (Where is the exit?)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in for a flight and get boarding pass
- Navigate to your gate using only German directions
- Handle a delayed flight situation and ask for information
- Go through immigration and answer basic questions

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Wo es das Gate?" → "Wo ist das Gate?" (correct verb position)
- "Ich spreche nicht Deutsch" → "Ich spreche kein Deutsch" (use "kein" not "nicht" before noun)
- "Mein Flug hat Delay" → "Mein Flug hat Verspätung" (use German word)
- "Kann ich das mitnehmen?" spoken too quietly → Speak clearly at security (they need to hear you!)
- "Reisepass, bitte" without "Entschuldigung" first → "Entschuldigung, mein Reisepass?" (be polite even when stressed)
- "Handgepäck" with wrong stress → "HAND-gepäck" (stress on first syllable)
- Forgetting to say "danke" to staff → Always say "danke" (basic politeness reduces stress)

## Cultural Notes

- **Arrive early**: German airports are efficient but thorough. Arrive 2-3 hours early for international flights. Security can take time despite efficiency.
- **Formal greetings matter**: Always greet staff with "Guten Tag" or "Guten Morgen" before making requests. Germans value formal politeness.
- **"Bitte" and "Danke" are essential**: Use "bitte" (please) and "danke" (thank you) constantly. Directness without these words seems rude.
- **Immigration questions**: Be prepared to answer "Zweck der Reise?" (purpose of trip) and "Wie lange bleiben Sie?" (how long are you staying). Keep answers brief and clear.
- **Baggage weight limits**: Limits are strictly enforced, especially by budget airlines like Ryanair and EasyJet. Know the limit before arriving.
- **Speaking English**: Major German airports (Frankfurt, Munich, Berlin) have English-speaking staff, but attempting German first shows respect and gets better service.
- **Boarding process**: German boarding is orderly and efficient. Listen for your zone or row number. Pushing ahead is frowned upon.
- **Security is thorough**: Liquid limits (100ml containers), no sharp objects, electronics out of bags. Follow rules precisely - Germans expect compliance.
- **Documents accessible**: Keep passport and boarding pass easily accessible. You'll show them multiple times (check-in, security, gate, boarding).
- **Punctuality**: Germans value punctuality. Arrive at gate early. Late arrivals may not be accommodated.
- **"Sie" vs "du"**: Use "Sie" (formal you) with all airport staff. "Du" is only for friends and family.
