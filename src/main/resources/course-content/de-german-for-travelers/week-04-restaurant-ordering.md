## This Week's Goals

- Order meals and drinks at restaurants and cafés

## Grammar Focus

### Ordering Food with "Ich möchte..." and "Ich hätte gern..."

**Rule:** Use "Ich möchte..." (I would like...) or "Ich hätte gern..." (I would like to have...) for polite ordering. These are your essential restaurant phrases.

Examples:
- "Ich möchte ein Bier, bitte" (I would like a beer, please)
- "Ich hätte gern die Bratwurst" (I would like the bratwurst)
- "Für mich bitte..." (For me please... - when ordering)
- "Einmal... bitte" (One... please - common ordering pattern)

### Asking Questions About Food

**Rule:** Use "Was ist...?" (What is...?) to ask about dishes and "Haben Sie...?" (Do you have...?) to ask about availability.

Examples:
- "Was ist das?" (What is that?)
- "Was empfehlen Sie?" (What do you recommend?)
- "Haben Sie vegetarische Gerichte?" (Do you have vegetarian dishes?)
- "Ist das scharf?" (Is that spicy?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Ordering a Meal at a Restaurant

Practice ordering appetizers, main courses, and drinks. Ask about the menu, request recommendations, and communicate preferences.

Key phrases:
- "Haben Sie einen Tisch für zwei Personen?" (Do you have a table for two?)
- "Die Speisekarte, bitte" (The menu, please)
- "Was empfehlen Sie?" (What do you recommend?)
- "Ich hätte gern das Schnitzel mit Pommes" (I'd like the schnitzel with fries)
- "Und für mich bitte den Sauerbraten" (And for me the sauerbraten, please)
- "Ein großes Bier, bitte" (A large beer, please)

### Asking About Ingredients and Dietary Restrictions

Practice asking what's in a dish, communicating allergies, and requesting modifications to accommodate dietary needs.

Key phrases:
- "Was ist in diesem Gericht?" (What's in this dish?)
- "Ist das vegetarisch?" (Is that vegetarian?)
- "Ich bin allergisch gegen Nüsse" (I'm allergic to nuts)
- "Haben Sie glutenfreie Optionen?" (Do you have gluten-free options?)
- "Ohne Zwiebeln, bitte" (Without onions, please)
- "Ist da Milch drin?" (Is there milk in it?)

### Paying the Bill

Practice requesting the bill, understanding the total, and handling payment with cash or card.

Key phrases:
- "Die Rechnung, bitte" (The bill, please)
- "Zahlen, bitte" (Pay, please)
- "Zusammen oder getrennt?" (Together or separate?)
- "Zusammen, bitte" (Together, please)
- "Kann ich mit Karte zahlen?" (Can I pay by card?)
- "Stimmt so" (Keep the change)

### Handling Problems or Special Requests

Practice asking for missing items, reporting incorrect orders, or making special requests politely.

Key phrases:
- "Das habe ich nicht bestellt" (I didn't order this)
- "Das Essen ist kalt" (The food is cold)
- "Können Sie das noch einmal bringen?" (Can you bring that again?)
- "Kann ich noch etwas Brot haben?" (Can I have some more bread?)
- "Wo ist die Toilette?" (Where is the bathroom?)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully order a complete meal (appetizer, main course, drink, dessert)
- Communicate dietary restrictions and verify ingredients
- Request the bill and pay correctly with appropriate tip
- Ask for recommendations and make a decision
- Handle an incorrect order politely and get it corrected

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Ich will..." → "Ich möchte..." or "Ich hätte gern..." (use polite forms, not "will")
- "Geben Sie mir..." → "Kann ich... haben, bitte?" (too direct - use polite request)
- "Ich habe Allergie zu Nüsse" → "Ich bin allergisch gegen Nüsse" (wrong preposition - use "gegen")
- Forgetting "bitte" when ordering → Always say "bitte" (essential politeness!)
- "Das Essen schmeckt" → "Das Essen schmeckt gut" (need adjective to complete thought)
- "Ein Wasser" without specifying → "Ein stilles Wasser" or "Ein Mineralwasser" (Germans will ask - specify preference)
- "Die Bill" → "Die Rechnung" (use German word, not English)
- Saying "Tip" in English → "Trinkgeld" or just "Stimmt so" (keep the change)
- Expecting waiter to bring bill automatically → Must request: "Die Rechnung, bitte" (waiters won't rush you)

## Cultural Notes

- **No free water**: In Germany, tap water is not typically served in restaurants. You must order bottled water (Mineralwasser = sparkling, Stilles Wasser = still). It's not free - it's charged like other drinks.
- **Sparkling vs still water**: Germans often prefer sparkling water (Mineralwasser). If you want still water, explicitly ask for "stilles Wasser" - otherwise you'll likely get sparkling.
- **Waiters won't rush you**: German waiters won't bring the bill unless you request it. They won't hover or pressure you to leave. This is considered respectful - you can stay as long as you want.
- **Request the bill**: Say "Die Rechnung, bitte" or "Zahlen, bitte" when ready to pay. Wave or make eye contact to get waiter's attention.
- **Tipping customs**: Service charge is included in German bills. Additional tipping is optional but appreciated. Round up or add 5-10% for good service. Say "Stimmt so" (keep the change) when handing payment.
- **Paying at the table**: In Germany, you typically pay the waiter at your table, not at a register. The waiter brings a card reader or takes cash directly.
- **Split bills are normal**: Germans often split bills ("getrennt zahlen"). Waiter will ask "Zusammen oder getrennt?" Don't be surprised if everyone pays separately.
- **Bread is not free**: Bread baskets on the table are usually charged per piece or basket. If you eat it, you pay for it.
- **Meal times**: Lunch (Mittagessen) is typically 12-2pm. Dinner (Abendessen) is 6-9pm. Many restaurants close between lunch and dinner service (3-5pm). Plan accordingly.
- **Reservations recommended**: For popular restaurants, especially in the evening, reservations are highly recommended. Walk-ins may face long waits.
- **"Mahlzeit"**: Around lunchtime, you may hear "Mahlzeit!" (literally "meal time") as a greeting. It's a casual greeting used around lunch hours in Germany.
- **German breakfast**: German breakfast (Frühstück) is typically bread rolls (Brötchen), cold cuts, cheese, jam, butter, coffee. Hotels often have extensive breakfast buffets.
- **Biergarten customs**: In traditional beer gardens, you can bring your own food but must buy drinks. Sharing tables with strangers is normal and expected when crowded.
- **"Prost" when toasting**: Always make eye contact when toasting ("Prost!" or "Zum Wohl!"). Not making eye contact is considered bad luck or disrespectful.
- **Stay seated to be served**: Don't go to the bar to order in sit-down restaurants. Wait for waiter to come to your table. In cafés, sometimes you order at counter - watch what others do.
