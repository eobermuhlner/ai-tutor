## This Week's Goals

- Use public transportation (trains, buses, trams, metro)
- Purchase tickets and understand fare systems
- Navigate stations and find correct platforms
- Take taxis and communicate destinations
- Ask for help when lost or confused

## Grammar Focus

### Expressing "I want to go to..."

**Rule:** Use "Ich möchte nach..." (I would like to go to...) for cities/countries and "Ich möchte zum/zur..." (I would like to go to the...) for specific places. This is your essential navigation phrase.

Examples:
- "Ich möchte nach Berlin" (I want to go to Berlin)
- "Ich möchte zum Flughafen" (I want to go to the airport)
- "Ich möchte zur Innenstadt" (I want to go to downtown)
- "Wo ist der Bahnhof?" (Where is the train station?)

### Asking About Schedules and Prices

**Rule:** Use "Wann...?" (When...?) for schedules and "Wie viel kostet...?" (How much does... cost?) for prices. These are survival questions for transportation.

Examples:
- "Wann fährt der nächste Zug?" (When does the next train leave?)
- "Wie viel kostet ein Ticket?" (How much does a ticket cost?)
- "Wie lange dauert die Fahrt?" (How long does the trip take?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Buying Train or Bus Tickets

Practice buying tickets at counters or machines, asking about prices, understanding fare zones, and validating tickets.

Key phrases:
- "Ich möchte nach [Stadt] fahren" (I want to go to [city])
- "Eine Einzelfahrkarte nach..., bitte" (A single ticket to..., please)
- "Wie viel kostet das?" (How much does that cost?)
- "Brauche ich umsteigen?" (Do I need to transfer?)
- "Von welchem Gleis fährt der Zug ab?" (Which platform does the train leave from?)
- "Muss ich das Ticket entwerten?" (Do I need to validate the ticket?)

### Navigating Public Transit

Practice finding the correct platform, asking about transfers, understanding announcements, and knowing when to get off.

Key phrases:
- "Welcher Bus fährt zum Zentrum?" (Which bus goes to the center?)
- "Wo ist Gleis 5?" (Where is platform 5?)
- "Ist das der Zug nach München?" (Is this the train to Munich?)
- "Wie viele Haltestellen sind es?" (How many stops is it?)
- "Wo muss ich aussteigen für...?" (Where do I get off for...?)
- "Fährt dieser Zug direkt?" (Does this train go direct?)

### Taking a Taxi or Rideshare

Practice hailing taxis, giving destination addresses, asking about fares, and handling payment.

Key phrases:
- "Zum Flughafen, bitte" (To the airport, please)
- "Wie viel kostet es ungefähr?" (How much does it cost approximately?)
- "Wie lange dauert die Fahrt?" (How long does the trip take?)
- "Können Sie hier anhalten?" (Can you stop here?)
- "Was macht das?" (How much is it? - final price)
- "Kann ich mit Karte zahlen?" (Can I pay by card?)

### Asking for Help When Lost

Practice asking for directions to stations, clarifying which line to take, and getting help from staff or locals.

Key phrases:
- "Entschuldigung, wo ist der nächste Bahnhof?" (Excuse me, where is the nearest station?)
- "Wie komme ich zum...?" (How do I get to...?)
- "Ich habe mich verirrt" (I'm lost)
- "Können Sie mir auf der Karte zeigen?" (Can you show me on the map?)
- "Welche Linie muss ich nehmen?" (Which line do I need to take?)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully buy a ticket for your destination and find the correct platform
- Navigate a transfer between two different lines or transportation types
- Take a taxi to a specific address and pay correctly
- Ask a local for help finding a station or stop
- Understand a delay announcement and ask about alternatives

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Ich will nach..." → "Ich möchte nach..." (use polite form "möchte" not "will")
- "Wie viel Zeit?" when asking duration → "Wie lange dauert es?" (use "wie lange" for duration)
- "Von wo?" → "Von welchem Gleis?" or "Von welcher Haltestelle?" (be specific)
- Forgetting to validate ticket → Always ask "Muss ich entwerten?" if unsure (fines are expensive!)
- "Der Bus zu Flughafen" → "Der Bus zum Flughafen" (zu + dem = zum)
- "Ich bin verloren" → "Ich habe mich verirrt" (more natural German)
- "Stopp hier" → "Halten Sie hier, bitte" or "Stoppen Sie hier" (use imperative correctly)
- Not saying "bitte" when making requests → Always add "bitte" (basic politeness)

## Cultural Notes

- **Tickets must be validated**: In Germany, you often buy tickets but must validate ("entwerten") them before boarding. Look for small yellow or red validation machines. Not validating = riding without a valid ticket = heavy fines.
- **Ticket inspectors**: Plain-clothes inspectors ("Kontrolleure") check tickets on trains, trams, and buses. Fines for invalid tickets are typically 60 euros. Always have a valid, validated ticket.
- **German efficiency and punctuality**: German public transportation is known for reliability. Trains/buses typically arrive on time. If you're late, you may miss your connection. Arrive early.
- **S-Bahn vs U-Bahn**: In cities like Berlin and Munich: S-Bahn (Stadtschnellbahn) is urban rail connecting suburbs. U-Bahn (Untergrundbahn) is metro/subway. Both use same ticket systems.
- **Fare zones**: German cities use zone systems (Zone A, B, C, etc.). Know your zones before buying tickets. Berlin, for example: Zone A = city center, Zone B = outer city, Zone C = outskirts including airport.
- **Day passes save money**: If taking 3+ trips in one day, "Tageskarte" (day pass) is usually cheaper than individual tickets.
- **Quiet zones on trains**: Some trains have "Ruhebereich" (quiet zone) where phone calls and loud talking are not allowed. Respect these zones.
- **Bicycle rules**: Bicycles allowed on many trains/trams but often require separate ticket. Check signage. Some trains don't allow bikes during rush hour.
- **Taxi regulations**: German taxis are regulated and metered. Expect fair pricing. Tipping is not mandatory but rounding up or adding 5-10% is appreciated.
- **Uber and alternatives**: Uber exists in major German cities but is less common than in the US. Local alternatives: FreeNow (formerly MyTaxi), Bolt. Traditional taxis are very reliable.
- **Asking locals for help**: Germans are generally helpful if you're polite. Always start with "Entschuldigung" (excuse me) and use "Sie" form. Many speak English but appreciate German attempts.
- **Punctuality expectations**: If meeting someone at a station, be on time. Germans value punctuality highly. Being late without notice is considered very rude.
