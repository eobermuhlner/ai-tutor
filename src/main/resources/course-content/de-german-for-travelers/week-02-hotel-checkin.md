## This Week's Goals

- Complete hotel check-in and check-out procedures
- Request specific room types and amenities
- Ask about hotel services and facilities
- Report room problems and request solutions
- Understand hotel policies and schedules

## Grammar Focus

### Expressing "I have" and "I need"

**Rule:** Use "Ich habe..." (I have...) for things you have and "Ich brauche..." (I need...) for things you need. These are essential hotel survival phrases.

Examples:
- "Ich habe eine Reservierung" (I have a reservation)
- "Ich habe eine Reservierung auf den Namen..." (I have a reservation under the name...)
- "Ich brauche ein Zimmer" (I need a room)
- "Ich brauche mehr Handtücher" (I need more towels)

### Asking About Availability and Services

**Rule:** Use "Haben Sie...?" (Do you have...?) and "Gibt es...?" (Is there...?) to ask about availability. Use "Wo ist...?" (Where is...?) for locations.

Examples:
- "Haben Sie freie Zimmer?" (Do you have rooms available?)
- "Gibt es WLAN?" (Is there WiFi?)
- "Ist das Frühstück inklusive?" (Is breakfast included?)
- "Wo ist der Fitnessraum?" (Where is the gym?)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In at the Hotel

Practice arriving at the hotel, confirming your reservation, providing identification, and getting your room key.

Key phrases:
- "Ich habe eine Reservierung auf den Namen [Name]" (I have a reservation under [Name])
- "Hier ist mein Reisepass" (Here is my passport)
- "Um wie viel Uhr gibt es Frühstück?" (What time is breakfast?)
- "Wo ist mein Zimmer?" (Where is my room?)
- "Wie lautet das WLAN-Passwort?" (What's the WiFi password?)

### Requesting Room Services and Amenities

Practice asking about hotel facilities, requesting extra items, and ordering room service.

Key phrases:
- "Wo ist das Schwimmbad?" (Where is the pool?)
- "Ich brauche mehr Handtücher" (I need more towels)
- "Können Sie mehr Kissen bringen?" (Can you bring more pillows?)
- "Haben Sie Zimmerservice?" (Do you have room service?)
- "Um wie viel Uhr schließt der Fitnessraum?" (What time does the gym close?)

### Reporting Room Problems

Practice reporting issues with your room (broken AC, no hot water, noise) and requesting solutions or room changes.

Key phrases:
- "Die Klimaanlage funktioniert nicht" (The air conditioning doesn't work)
- "Es gibt kein warmes Wasser" (There's no hot water)
- "Das Zimmer ist sehr schmutzig" (The room is very dirty)
- "Es ist sehr laut, ich kann nicht schlafen" (It's very noisy, I can't sleep)
- "Können Sie mir ein anderes Zimmer geben?" (Can you give me another room?)
- "Wann können Sie es reparieren?" (When can you fix it?)

### Checking Out and Extending Stay

Practice reviewing your bill, checking out, arranging late checkout, or extending your stay.

Key phrases:
- "Ich möchte auschecken" (I would like to check out)
- "Kann ich die Rechnung sehen?" (Can I see the bill?)
- "Was ist diese Gebühr?" (What is this charge?)
- "Kann ich meine Koffer hier lassen?" (Can I leave my suitcases here?)
- "Ich möchte noch eine Nacht bleiben" (I want to stay one more night)

## Practice Patterns

- Successfully check in and get to your room with WiFi password
- Request additional room items or services
- Report a room problem and negotiate a solution or room change
- Extend your stay by one or more nights
- Check out, review bill, and arrange luggage storage

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- "Ich habe Reservierung" → "Ich habe eine Reservierung" (need article "eine")
- "Blick auf das Meer" → "Meerblick" (German uses compound noun)
- "Haben Sie verfügbar?" → "Haben Sie freie Zimmer?" or "Gibt es noch Zimmer?" (complete the phrase)
- "Ich brauche zwei Handtuch" → "Ich brauche zwei Handtücher" (plural)
- "Das WLAN geht nicht" → "Das WLAN funktioniert nicht" (use "funktionieren" for technology)
- "Es gibt viel Lärm" when complaining → "Es ist sehr laut" (more natural)
- "Wie viel kostet die Nacht?" → "Wie viel kostet es pro Nacht?" (use "pro Nacht")
- Forgetting "bitte" when requesting items → Always add "bitte" (basic politeness)

## Cultural Notes

- **Reservation confirmation**: Always carry printed or digital confirmation. Germans are organized and expect documentation.
- **Passport required**: Hotels legally must record your passport information in Germany. This is normal procedure.
- **Check-in/check-out times**: Check-in usually 2-3pm, check-out 10-11am. Early check-in may cost extra. Late check-out depends on availability.
- **Breakfast times**: Hotel breakfast often 7-10am. German breakfast is substantial: bread rolls, cold cuts, cheese, eggs, coffee.
- **Room cleaning**: Housekeeping typically cleans before 2pm. Hang "Bitte nicht stören" (Do not disturb) sign if sleeping late.
- **WiFi**: Most hotels have WLAN (WiFi), quality is usually good. Ask for password at check-in: "Wie lautet das WLAN-Passwort?"
- **Deposits**: Some hotels require cash deposit or credit card authorization. Ensure you understand charges before checking in.
- **Tipping**: Not mandatory in Germany but appreciated. Leave 1-2 euros per night for housekeeping. Bellhops: 1-2 euros per bag.
- **Safe usage**: Use room safe for passport and valuables. Hotel generally not liable for items left in room.
- **Minibar charges**: Minibar items are expensive. Check prices before consuming. Often cheaper to buy at nearby store.
- **Noise complaints**: If room is noisy, request room change immediately, not at checkout. Hotels can usually accommodate.
- **Problem resolution**: Report problems immediately, not at checkout. Germans appreciate directness - be clear about issues.
- **Formal language**: Use "Sie" form (formal) with all hotel staff. "Du" form is only for friends and family.
