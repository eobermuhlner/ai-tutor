## Grammar Focus

### Prepositions of Place

**Rule:** Prepositions indicate location and direction

Location:
- À (at/to): à gauche (on the left), à droite (on the right)
- Sur (on): sur la table (on the table)
- Dans (in): dans la rue (in the street)
- Devant (in front of): devant l'église (in front of the church)
- Derrière (behind): derrière le musée (behind the museum)
- Entre (between): entre la banque et la poste (between the bank and post office)
- En face de (across from): en face de l'hôtel (across from the hotel)
- À côté de (next to): à côté du parc (next to the park)
- Près de (near): près de la gare (near the station)
- Loin de (far from): loin du centre (far from downtown)

### The Imperative Mood (Commands)

**Rule:** Use imperative to give directions or instructions

Formation - remove the subject pronoun:
- Tu vas → Va! (Go!)
- Vous allez → Allez! (Go! - formal/plural)
- Tu tournes → Tourne! (Turn!)
- Vous tournez → Tournez! (Turn! - formal/plural)
- Tu continues → Continue! (Continue!)
- Vous continuez → Continuez! (Continue! - formal/plural)

**Note:** -er verbs drop the -s in tu form
- Tu prends → Prends! (Take!)
- BUT: Tu vas → Va! (not "Vas!")

Examples for directions:
- Tournez à gauche (Turn left - formal)
- Allez tout droit (Go straight)
- Continuez jusqu'à la Place (Continue until the square)
- Prenez la première rue à droite (Take the first street on the right)

### The Verb ALLER (to go)

**Rule:** Aller is irregular and essential for directions

Conjugation:
- Je vais (I go)
- Tu vas (You go)
- Il/Elle va (He/She goes)
- Nous allons (We go)
- Vous allez (You go)
- Ils/Elles vont (They go)

Examples:
- Je vais à la gare (I'm going to the station)
- Comment je vais à l'hôtel? (How do I get to the hotel?)
- Vous allez tout droit (You go straight)

Topics to cover:
- "Excusez-moi, où est la gare?" (Excuse me, where is the station?)

Practice giving clear directions using the imperative and prepositions.

Topics to cover:
- "Allez tout droit, puis tournez à gauche" (Go straight, then turn left)
- "Prenez la deuxième rue à droite" (Take the second street on the right)
- "C'est en face de la banque" (It's across from the bank)
- "Continuez jusqu'au bout de la rue" (Continue to the end of the street)

### Describing Locations

Practice describing where places are relative to each other.

Topics to cover:
- "La poste est à côté de la banque" (The post office is next to the bank)
- "Le musée est derrière l'église" (The museum is behind the church)
- "Le café est entre l'hôtel et le restaurant" (The café is between the hotel and restaurant)

## Practice Patterns

- Ask "Où est...?" for 5 different places
- Practice giving directions with commands: Allez, Tournez, Prenez
- Use prepositions: à gauche, à droite, tout droit, en face de
- Describe locations using à côté de, entre, derrière, devant
- Practice both asking and giving directions for the same route
- Use "Comment je vais à...?" with different destinations

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- Forgetting "de" with certain prepositions: "En face l'hôtel" → "En face de l'hôtel"
- Adding "-s" to tu imperative of -er verbs: "Tournes" → "Tourne"
- Wrong preposition: "Sur la gauche" → "À gauche"
- Saying "gauche" alone without "à": "Tournez gauche" → "Tournez à gauche"
- Using present instead of imperative: "Vous tournez" (you turn) vs. "Tournez!" (turn!)
- Confusing près/prêt: près de (near) vs. prêt (ready)

## Cultural Notes

- French cities often have boulevards, avenues, and places (squares)
- Many streets are named after historical figures or dates
- "Métro" is the Paris subway system - 16 lines
- In Paris, the Seine river divides "Rive Gauche" (Left Bank) and "Rive Droite" (Right Bank)
- French people will often walk with you if you're very lost
- GPS addresses in France include arrondissement (district) in cities
