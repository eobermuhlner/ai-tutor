## Grammar Focus

### Demonstrative Adjectives (This/That/These/Those)

**Rule:** Demonstratives must agree in gender and number with the noun

| Gender/Number | Before Consonant | Before Vowel/H | Feminine | Plural |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|----------|---------|
| Masculine | ce | cet | cette | ces |

Examples:
- Ce pantalon (This pants - masculine, consonant)
- Cet homme (This man - masculine, vowel)
- Cette robe (This dress - feminine)
- Ces chaussures (These shoes - plural)

**Adding -ci or -là for emphasis:**
- Ce pantalon-ci (This pants here - close)
- Ce pantalon-là (That pants there - farther)

### Color Adjectives

**Rule:** Color adjectives agree in gender and number

Regular colors (add -e for feminine, -s for plural):
- Noir/e (black), blanc/blanche (white), vert/e (green), gris/e (gray)
- Un pantalon noir, une chemise noire, des chaussures noires

Invariable colors (don't change):
- Orange, marron, beige
- Une chemise orange (not "orangée")

Special patterns:
- Rouge: rouge/rouge/rouges/rouges (same masc/fem)
- Bleu: bleu/bleue/bleus/bleues
- Violet: violet/violette/violets/violettes

### The Verb ACHETER (to buy)

**Rule:** Acheter has spelling changes with accent grave

Conjugation:
- J'achète (I buy)
- Tu achètes (You buy)
- Il/Elle achète (He/She buys)
- Nous achetons (We buy)
- Vous achetez (You buy)
- Ils/Elles achètent (They buy)

**Note:** Accent grave (è) appears before silent endings

Examples:
- J'achète une robe (I'm buying a dress)
- Qu'est-ce que tu achètes? (What are you buying?)
- Nous achetons des chaussures (We're buying shoes)

### Asking Prices: Combien

**Rule:** Use "Combien" to ask "How much"

- Combien ça coûte? (How much does it cost?)
- Combien coûte cette chemise? (How much does this shirt cost?)
- Combien coûtent ces chaussures? (How much do these shoes cost?)
- C'est combien? (How much is it? - informal)

## Practice Patterns

- Use demonstratives correctly: ce/cet/cette/ces
- Practice color agreement: un pantalon noir, une robe noire, des chaussures noires
- Ask prices with "Combien ça coûte?" and "Combien coûte/coûtent?"
- Conjugate acheter with all pronouns
- Describe items using adjectives: grand, petit, beau, cher
- Practice saying sizes: Je fais du 40, Je chausse du 42

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- Wrong demonstrative: "Cet chemise" → "Cette chemise" (chemise is feminine)
- Wrong color agreement: "Une chemise noir" → "Une chemise noire"
- Forgetting -ci/-là: Use only when emphasizing (not always needed)
- Invariable colors changing: "Une robe marrone" → "Une robe marron"
- Wrong verb for size: "Je suis du 38" → "Je fais du 38"
- Plural confusion: "Combien coûte ces chaussures?" → "Combien coûtent ces chaussures?"

## Cultural Notes

- French clothing sizes are different: EU 38 ≈ US 6
- French shoe sizes: EU 39 ≈ US 8 (women), EU 42 ≈ US 9 (men)
- Sales (les soldes) happen twice a year: January and July
- In boutiques, greet with "Bonjour" when entering
- French fashion emphasizes quality over quantity
- "Prêt-à-porter" means ready-to-wear (standard sizes)
