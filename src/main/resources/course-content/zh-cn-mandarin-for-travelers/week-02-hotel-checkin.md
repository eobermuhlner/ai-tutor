## Grammar Focus

### Verb-Object Complements (Verb + Result Complement)
**Rule:** Verb + 结果补语 (result complement) to express completion or result of an action

**Common complements:**
- **到** (dào) - to reach/completion
- **见** (jiàn) - to see
- **完** (wán) - to finish/complete
- **好** (hǎo) - to be good/right

**Examples:**
- **听懂** (tīng dǒng) - to understand (what is heard)
- **学会** (xué huì) - to learn/master
- **看见** (kàn jiàn) - to see
- **拿到** (ná dào) - to get/have in hand

**Sentence structure:** Subject + Verb + Object + Complement

**Examples:**
- 我**听懂**了。(Wǒ tīng dǒng le.) - I understood.
- 你**学会**了吗？(Nǐ xué huì le ma?) - Have you learned it?

### Expressing Existence with 有 (yǒu) and 没有 (méiyǒu)
**Rule:** Use 有 for "there is/are" and 没有 for "there isn't/aren't"

**Structure:** Place/Time + 有 + Object

**Examples:**
- **酒店有**餐厅吗？(Jiǔdiàn yǒu cāntīng ma?) - Does the hotel have a restaurant?
- **房间里有**电视吗？(Fángjiān lǐ yǒu diànshì ma?) - Is there a TV in the room?

### Expressing Likes and Preferences with 喜欢 (xǐhuān)
**Rule:** Subject + 喜欢 + Object

**Examples:**
- 我**喜欢**中国菜。(Wǒ xǐhuān Zhōngguó cài.) - I like Chinese food.
- 他**不喜欢**喝茶。(Tā bù xǐhuān hē chá.) - He doesn't like drinking tea.

## Essential Vocabulary

### Hotel Reception
- **前台** (qiántái) - Front desk
- **房间** (fángjiān) - Room
- **钥匙** (yàoshi) - Key
- **房卡** (fángkǎ) - Room key card
- **前台服务员** (qiántái fúwùyuán) - Front desk clerk
- **预订** (yùdìng) - Reservation

### Room Types
- **单人房** (dānrén fáng) - Single room
- **双人房** (shuāngrén fáng) - Double room
- **三人房** (sānrén fáng) - Triple room
- **套房** (tàofáng) - Suite
- **空调** (kōngtiáo) - Air conditioning
- **暖气** (nuǎnqì) - Heating

### Amenities
- **浴室** (yùshì) - Bathroom
- **淋浴** (línyù) - Shower
- **电话** (diànhuà) - Phone
- **电视** (diànshì) - TV
- **无线网络** (wúxiàn wǎngluò) - Internet/Wi-Fi
- **保险箱** (bǎoxiǎnxiāng) - Safe

### Common Phrases
- **我有预订** (Wǒ yǒu yùdìng) - I have a reservation
- **还有空房吗**？(Hái yǒu kòngfáng ma?) - Do you have availability?
- **一晚多少钱**？(Yī wǎn duōshǎo qián?) - How much per night?
- **早餐包括吗**？(Zǎocān bāokuò ma?) - Is breakfast included?

## Conversation Scenarios

### Checking In
**Guest:** 您好，我有预订。
**Receptionist:** 请问您叫什么名字？
**Guest:** 我叫[Name]，我预订了一个双人房住两晚。
**Receptionist:** 有的，给您。您要海景房吗？
**Guest:** 要的，太好了。早餐是几点？

### Asking for Services
**Guest:** 早餐包括吗？
**Receptionist:** 包括的，早上7点到10点在一楼餐厅供应。
**Guest:** 健身房在哪里？
**Receptionist:** 健身房在三楼。

## Practice Patterns

- Form sentences using verb-result complements
- Ask about existence using 有/没有
- Express preferences using 喜欢
- Request specific room features

## Common Mistakes to Watch

- Wrong form → Correct form (explanation)
- "我学会了" ✓ (correct use of result complement)
- "酒店有餐厅吗"？✓ (correct existence question)
- "我喜欢中国菜" ✓ (correct preference expression)
- "我拿到了钥匙" ✓ (correct completion with 到)
- "还有空房吗"？✓ (correct questioning with 还)
- "早餐包括吗"？✓ (correct omission of 是 in questions)

## Cultural Notes

- Hotel check-ins in China are formal with emphasis on efficiency
- Tipping is generally not expected or accepted in mainland China
- Breakfast ("早餐") in Chinese hotels often includes congee, steamed buns, and tea
- Chinese hotel staff are typically very service-oriented and eager to help
- When checking in, be prepared to show your passport and possibly scan fingerprints
- Many hotels provide "一次性用品" (yīcìxìng yòngpǐn) - disposable amenities like toothbrushes and slippers
